The Traits of the Houses





Gryffindor's Traits

The Gryffindor house emphasises the traits of courage as well as daring, nerve, and chivalry, and thus its members are generally regarded as brave, though sometimes to the point of recklessness. They can also be short-tempered, and even do things without thinking. Because of these traits, their symbol is the lion, which naturally represents all the traist stated for a Gryffindor.









Slytherin's Traits

The Slytherin house is well known for being quite clever and resourceful, along with their amazing abilties to be leaders. Some members of the house may be a bit bossy because of this, but they are dependable when you need them. They will never leave their own behind, so they have quite an efficient way of teamwork.








Ravenclaw Traits

Members of this house are characterized by their wit, learning, and wisdom. Ravenclaw corresponds roughly to the element of air, which is a given, since they represent the eagle. They're well-known for their originality, as well as their creativity. In fact, a good example of a Ravenclaw is Hermione, who was suggested to join the Ravenclaw house.








HufflePuff Traits

Hufflepuff is the most inclusive among the four houses; valuing hard work, dedication, patience, loyalty, and fair play. Unlike other houses, they're not as competitive in their daily lives, and they tend to be more modest and introverted. In a quidditch match however, be prepared - they're extremely competitive in this instance.
